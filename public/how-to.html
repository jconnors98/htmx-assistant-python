Step 1

General Setup

Start by defining how your Assistant appears and behaves: its name, branding, core instructions, and the first experience users see when they open it.

Internal Name, Display Title & Colours

These settings control how your Assistant appears in the Mode Editor and for end users.

  • Internal Mode Name – unique ID (e.g., “support-bot”), you will not be able to edit this
  • Display Title – what users see (e.g., “Ask HR” or “Program Assistant”)
  • Colours – match your brand’s primary colour so the Assistant feels native

Welcome Message

The welcome message is the first text users see above the chat box. Use it to explain who the Assistant is and what it can help with.

  • “Hi, I’m your Programs Assistant. Ask me about eligibility, deadlines, or how to apply.”
  • “I can help you find training, funding, and support options based on your situation.”

Description (System Prompt)

Labeled as Description in the editor, this text is injected into the AI's System Prompt. It defines the Assistant's job description and rules.

  • Who the Assistant represents (site, organization, program)
  • What topics it should answer and what to avoid
  • Any required disclaimers or boundaries

A clear description keeps answers consistent with your policies.

Quick Prompts (Common Questions)

Quick prompts appear as clickable suggestions under the welcome message. They help users get started and discover what your Assistant can do.

  • “What programs are available right now?”
  • “Where do I find apprenticeship or training information?”
  • “What funding or grants might apply to me?”
  • “How do I register or apply?”

You can edit these at any time. Changes are live instantly for all new conversations.

Step 2

Website & PDF Scraping

Scraping imports content from your websites and PDFs so your Assistant can answer from trusted, publicly visible information—without you copying and pasting text.

Website scraping

When enabled, website scraping will:

  • Read every publicly visible page you’ve connected
  • Capture pop-ups, expandable “+” sections, and JavaScript-driven content
  • Incrementally update only new or changed content
  • Allow you to include partner sites alongside your own
  • Reuse an existing partner Mode’s scraping if they already have one, so the system does not re-scrape the same site

Scraping Frequency

You can control how often the Assistant updates its knowledge:

  • Manual – only scrapes when you click "Run Now"
  • Daily – updates automatically every day (2 AM UTC)
  • Weekly – updates automatically every week (Sunday 3 AM UTC)

PDF scraping from your sites

As your sites are scraped, PDFs linked on your pages are automatically discovered and listed for review.

  • Discovered PDFs appear in a PDF list under Scraping or Discovered Files
  • You choose which PDFs to enable for this Assistant
  • Only enabled PDFs are indexed and available to answer questions

Enabled PDFs then appear in the Documents section (Step 3), where you can manage them alongside uploaded files.

Managing scraped pages

You can exclude any scraped page so the Assistant will not use it in responses.

  • Exclude pages that are outdated, confusing, or not meant for this audience
  • Exclusion affects only this Mode—your site stays unchanged

If a page appears in Scraped Pages it was already public. To remove it everywhere (including search and future scraping), you must unpublish or restrict it in your CMS.

Step 3

Documents

Use the Documents section to upload files and manage any scraped PDFs you’ve enabled. This is where you control exactly which documents your Assistant can reference.

Upload your own documents

Upload files that don’t live on your public website or that you want tightly controlled.

  • Internal HR or policy manuals
  • Training guides and onboarding materials
  • Partner agreements or program descriptions
  • Legacy PDFs not currently on your site

Uploaded documents appear in your Documents list, where you can download or delete them at any time.

Scraped PDFs you’ve enabled

Any “enabled” PDFs from Step 2 are also listed in Documents. This gives you a single place to see all files the Assistant is using.

  • Review which PDFs from your sites and partners are active
  • Remove a PDF from the Assistant by disabling or deleting it
  • Keep a clear record of which documents power your answers
Step 4

Advanced Settings

Advanced settings control where the Assistant looks for answers first, if it’s allowed to use the internet, which sites to exclude, and how to safely delete a Mode.

Search priority

Decide whether the Assistant should look at your websites or documents first.

  • Websites first – best when your public site is the single source of truth.
  • Documents first – best for internal HR, policy, or training Assistants.

The Assistant still considers all allowed sources—it simply changes which content is evaluated first.

Internet access & blocked websites

Internet access lets the Assistant look beyond your content when needed.

Allow general internet search
  • Assistant checks your content first
  • Then optionally searches the wider internet

You can also maintain a list of blocked websites—domains the Assistant must never use, even if internet search is enabled.

!
Delete Mode (Permanent)

Deleting a Mode permanently removes its configuration and access. You’ll be asked to confirm twice. Once deleted, a Mode cannot be restored—but you can always create a new one using similar settings.

Step 5

Analytics

Analytics lets you see how people are using your Assistant: what they ask, how it responds, and where you might need to improve your content.

How to open Analytics

  • Go to the Admin Dashboard (Mode List)
  • Click the graph icon on the Mode card
  • Analytics opens in a dedicated view

From there, you can:

  • View every conversation and message
  • See exactly what users asked and how the AI replied
  • Filter by date, topic, or Mode
  • Ask natural language questions about your data (e.g., “What topics came up most this month?”)
Help

Frequently asked questions

Does scraping affect SEO or change my site?

No. Scraping only reads publicly visible content. It doesn’t modify your website or how search engines see it.

Can I control what the Assistant knows?

Yes. You decide which pages, PDFs, and uploaded documents are included and whether internet search is allowed.

How often should I re-scrape my sites?

Re-scrape after making significant content updates. The system only adds new or changed content.

Can I have different Assistants for different audiences?

Yes. Create multiple Modes—for example, a public Programs Assistant and a private HR Assistant—with different content and rules.
